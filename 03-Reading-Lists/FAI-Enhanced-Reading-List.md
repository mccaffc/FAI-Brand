# FAI Brand: Enhanced Reading List
## Comprehensive Guide to Design Systems, Swiss Modernism, and Brand Architecture

*Compiled February 2026*

---

## Introduction

This enhanced reading list expands on the foundational FAI Design Reading List, providing direct links, current resources, interactive tools, and organized pathways for learning. Whether you're a designer, brand strategist, or organizational leader, this guide offers multiple entry points into the concepts that underpin FAI's visual and strategic identity.

The list is organized by topic area, with each resource categorized by type, difficulty level, cost, and time commitment. Use the Quick Start sections to begin immediately, or dive deep into specific topics that resonate with your role.

---

## Quick Start: 2-Hour Reading List

Start here for the essential foundation. These five resources provide the core concepts in minimal time.

### Must-Read (2 hours total)

1. **Tim Brown – "More Meaningful Typography"** (Free Article, 15 min)
   - Link: [A List Apart](https://alistapart.com/article/more-meaningful-typography/)
   - Type: Article
   - Cost: Free
   - Difficulty: Beginner
   - Why: Introduces modular type scales and the musical interval concept that undergirds FAI's pentatonic scale
   - Key insight: Type sizes should relate proportionally, not arbitrarily

2. **Refactoring UI – Color Chapter** (Free Preview, 30 min)
   - Link: [Building Your Color Palette](https://refactoringui.com/previews/building-your-color-palette/)
   - Type: Online Guide (Book excerpt)
   - Cost: Free preview / $99 full book
   - Difficulty: Beginner
   - Why: Practical method for building systematic color scales with tints and shades
   - Key insight: Adjust hue and saturation at each step, not just lightness

3. **Josef Müller-Brockmann – Grid Systems in Graphic Design** (Book, 60 min critical reading)
   - Link: [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Grid-systems-graphic-design-communication/dp/3721201450)
   - ISBN: 3721201450
   - Type: Book
   - Cost: ~$40
   - Difficulty: Intermediate
   - Year: 1981 (original), reprinted 2015
   - Time to complete: 4-6 hours (focused reading possible in 60 min)
   - Why: The foundational text. Every visual principle in FAI's brand descends from grid logic
   - Key chapters: Chapters 1-3 (theory and basic grid systems)
   - Availability: Physical book, some libraries, [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/GridSystemsInGraphicDesignJosefMullerBrockmann)

4. **Spencer Mortensen – Interactive Modular Scale Tool** (Free Tool, 15 min)
   - Link: [type-scale.com](https://www.modularscale.com/)
   - Type: Interactive Tool
   - Cost: Free
   - Difficulty: Beginner
   - Why: Play with FAI's pentatonic scale (36pt – 21pt – 12pt – 11pt – 10pt) to understand proportional relationships
   - How to use: Plug in 11pt as base, experiment with different ratios to match FAI's scale

5. **IBM Design Language – Typography Guide** (Free Online, 30 min)
   - Link: [ibm.com/design/language/typography](https://www.ibm.com/design/language/typography/typeface/)
   - Type: Online Documentation
   - Cost: Free
   - Difficulty: Beginner
   - Why: Live example of how IBM Plex works in a professional design system; shows the grid logic applied to real-world typography
   - Key insight: Plex was designed to work within IBM's 2x grid system

**Total time: ~2 hours. You'll understand type scales, grids, and color systems.**

## Weekend Deep Dive: 2-3 Day Learning Path

For comprehensive understanding, combine focused reading with hands-on practice.

### Day 1: Foundations (8 hours)

**Morning (4 hours)**
- Read: Müller-Brockmann's *Grid Systems* (full engagement) + optional monograph
- Practice: Sketch a 12-column grid on paper; understand why constraints enable communication
- Explore: [The Swiss Grid exhibition resource](https://swissgrid.posterhouse.org/) (30 min)

**Afternoon (4 hours)**
- Read: Bringhurst's *Elements of Typographic Style*, Chapter 8 ("Shaping the Page")
- Read: Tim Brown's modular scale article + complementary [Envato guide](https://webdesign.tutsplus.com/how-to-establish-a-modular-typographic-scale--webdesign-14927a)
- Practice: Use [type-scale.com](https://www.modularscale.com/) to generate type scales; compare to FAI's

### Day 2: Systems & Color (8 hours)

**Morning (4 hours)**
- Read: Refactoring UI color chapter (complete)
- Read: Josef Albers's *Interaction of Color* (or [interactive edition](https://interactionofcolor.com/))
- Practice: Use [Color Designer](https://colordesigner.io/) or [HSL Color Picker](https://www.hslpicker.com/) to build a shade/tint system from scratch

**Afternoon (4 hours)**
- Read: Karl Gerstner's *Designing Programmes* (introduction and case studies)
- Explore: [IBM Plex Design Philosophy](https://www.ibm.com/design/impact/plex/)
- Practice: Study FAI's illustration system; trace how base shapes generate compositions

### Day 3: Strategy & Context (6-8 hours)

**Morning (4 hours)**
- Read: Paul Rand's *A Designer's Art* (or key essays online)
- Read: Richard Hollis's *Swiss Graphic Design: The Origins and Growth* (or summary article from [Smashing Magazine](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2009/07/lessons-from-swiss-style-graphic-design/))
- Context: Understand why Swiss design emerged and why it still matters

**Afternoon (2-4 hours)**
- Read: David Aaker's *Brand Portfolio Strategy* (Introduction and Chapter 1 on brand architecture)
- Explore: Michael Bierut's *How to* (case studies on systems thinking)
- Synthesis: Map FAI's multi-brand structure onto Aaker's framework

## Glossary of Key Concepts (Quick Reference)

**Brand Architecture**: The structure organizing multiple brands under one parent (e.g., Aaker's framework)

**Branded House**: Single master brand with sub-brands/products beneath it (e.g., IBM)

**Design System**: Complete set of reusable components, patterns, and guidelines (e.g., Material Design)

**Grid System**: Underlying structural framework organizing space systematically (Swiss tradition)

**Modular Scale**: Type sizes that relate proportionally to each other (musical intervals)

**Pentatonic Scale**: Set of exactly 5 distinct sizes (FAI uses 36–21–12–11–10pt)

**Shades & Tints**: Color variations (tints = color + white; shades = color + black)

**Swiss International Style**: Post-WWII design movement emphasizing grids, sans-serif type, high contrast, and systematic thinking

**Token**: Reusable design value (color, size, spacing) that scales across applications
