# FAI Brand & Design System

## Who FAI Is

FAI is a tech-policy think tank bridging Silicon Valley and Washington. Its brand lives in productive tension between **institutional gravity** (cabinet secretaries, major foundation funders, Senate committees) and **startup energy** (Silicon Valley DNA, raves at DC nightclubs, "move fast and build things"). Every design decision should hold both of these at once — never collapsing into stuffy think-tank conservatism, nor into ephemeral startup casualness.

**Taglines:**
- "No Innovation without Representation" — institutional, slightly polemical
- "Build Tech. Promote Freedom." — action-oriented, startup energy
- "Move fast and build things." — internal mantra

**Mission:** Champion the technology, talent, and ideas essential to American prosperity, security, and flourishing.

## COLOR SYSTEM

### Primary Palette (non-negotiable)

| Name | Hex | RGB | CMYK | Pantone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **International Orange** | `#FF4F00` | 255, 79, 0 | C0 M84 Y100 K0 | 021 C |
| **Cod Gray** | `#121212` | 17, 17, 17 | C0 M0 Y0 K93 | Black 6 C |
| **Pure White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | C0 M0 Y0 K0 | White |

**Critical notes on International Orange:** This is not red, vermillion, or burnt orange. It reads closer to a hazard sign than a sunset — saturated, forward-leaning, slightly dangerous. The exact hex `#FF4F00` must be used. Do not approximate.

**Critical note on Cod Gray:** This is not true black (#000000). It is a warm near-black that's softer on screen and gives dark backgrounds their particular character.

### Secondary Palette (illustrations, data visualization, supporting graphics)

| Name | Hex | RGB | CMYK | Pantone |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Chrome Yellow** | `#FFA300` | 255, 163, 0 | C0 M36 Y100 K0 | 137 C |
| **Celestial Blue** | `#4997D0` | 73, 151, 208 | C65 M28 Y0 K18 | 7688 C |
| **Timberwolf** | `#D9D9D6` | 217, 217, 214 | C0 M0 Y1 K15 | Cool Gray 1 C |

Secondary colors appear primarily in the illustration system and data visualizations. They are not as tightly codified for document body use.

### Color Usage Rules

**Backgrounds:**
- **Cod Gray** (`#121212`) — statement pages, cover pages, impact/section-divider pages, presentations. This is the "FAI dark mode" that gives the brand its authority and edge.
- **Pure White** (`#FFFFFF`) — body content pages, research papers, reports, most publication interior pages.
- **International Orange** (`#FF4F00`) — section chapter-opener pages (used as full-bleed background). Use sparingly and deliberately.

**Text on backgrounds:**
- White text on Cod Gray ✅
- Cod Gray text on White ✅
- Cod Gray text (or black) on International Orange ✅
- White text on International Orange ✅ (for large text only; contrast is borderline at small sizes)
- **Never:** mix mark and logotype in different colors

**Accent use of International Orange:**
- Section divider bars and rules
- Pull quote highlights or key words within a title
- Primary data series in charts
- Callout boxes or emphasis elements

## TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### The Three Typeface Families

**1. Schmalfette Grotesk** — Display/Impact only
- Ultra-condensed grotesque
- Used for: logotype, large section headers, chapter openers, event titles, poster-scale headlines
- Settings: optical kerning, +20 tracking, ALL CAPS
- Limit to 2–3 lines and 2–3 words per line
- Does NOT work for running text or anything below ~36pt
- Licensed — contact Chris McCaffery for font access

**2. IBM Plex Serif** — Primary workhorse
- Used for: headings, subheadings, body text, pull quotes in all publications and documents
- Available free from IBM's GitHub or Google Fonts
- This is what people read when reading FAI publications

**3. IBM Plex Sans / Sans Condensed / Mono** — Utility and support
- **IBM Plex Sans:** General UI, captions, supporting text, internal documentation headings
- **IBM Plex Sans Condensed:** Footnotes, space-constrained contexts, subheading 2 (all caps)
- **IBM Plex Mono:** Blockquotes, specific pull headings in publications

### The Pentatonic Type Scale

FAI uses a pentatonic scale — five predetermined sizes derived from the formula `an = a0 × r^(n-1)` where base is 12pt. Like five notes in a musical scale, any combination looks intentional.

**Core five "notes" for standard documents:**

| Size | Line-height | Letter-spacing | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **36pt** | 120% (43.2pt) | -1% (-0.01em) | Pull quotes, major callouts |
| **21pt** | 130% (27.3pt) | 0% | Subheads, section titles |
| **12pt** | 140% (16.8pt) | 0% | Headings (testimonies/letters) |
| **11pt** | 140% (15.4pt) | 0% | Body text |
| **10pt** | 140% (14pt) | 0% | Captions, footnotes |

**Extended scale reference (IBM Plex Serif):**

| Size | Line-height | Letter-spacing |
|---|---|---|
| 9–18pt | 140% | 0% |
| 21–28pt | 130% | 0% |
| 32pt | 130% | -1% (-0.01em) |
| 36–55pt | 120% | -1% (-0.01em) |
| 64–84pt | 110% | -2% (-0.02em) |
| 96pt | 110% | -2% (-0.02em) |

Note: IBM Plex Mono uses 0% letter-spacing at all sizes.

## LOGO SYSTEM

### Components

**1. Logomark (double chevron)** — Two right-pointing chevrons, reading as a "fast forward" symbol. Represents FAI's relentless forward momentum. Used standalone when FAI name is already established in context.

**2. Logotype** — "FOUNDATION FOR / AMERICAN INNOVATION" in Schmalfette Grotesk, all caps, stacked on two lines, carefully kerned. Do not recreate with live type — use the locked asset.

**3. Full Lockup** — Logomark + logotype together. This is the primary, most widely used version for external-facing applications.

**4. Acronym lockup** — Double chevron + "FAI" text. Used where space is limited or the full name is redundant.

### Logo Asset Files

All logo SVGs live in `FAI-Logo-Assets/` (sibling folder to this SKILL.md). Six files — two color variants of three logo shapes. All have transparent backgrounds; the document or layout controls the background. **Always use these files — never recreate from scratch.**

| File | Use on... |
|---|---|
| `Lockup-Black.svg` | White or orange backgrounds |
| `Lockup-White.svg` | Cod Gray / dark backgrounds |
| `Acronym-Black.svg` | White or orange backgrounds |
| `Acronym-White.svg` | Cod Gray / dark backgrounds |
| `Logomark-Black.svg` | White or orange backgrounds |
| `Logomark-White.svg` | Cod Gray / dark backgrounds |

The rule is simple: **dark background → White file. Light or orange background → Black file.**

#### SVG viewBox dimensions (for layout calculations)

| File group | viewBox | Aspect ratio |
|---|---|---|
| Logomark | `0 0 900 1081` | ~0.83:1 (portrait, tall) |
| Acronym | `0 0 1910 1003` | ~1.9:1 (horizontal) |
| Lockup | `0 0 1920 425` | ~4.5:1 (wide horizontal) |

### Three Approved Color Combinations

| Combination | Mark color | Background | Use case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Combination 1** | Black (`#010101`) | International Orange (`#FF4F00`) | High-energy applications, section openers |
| **Combination 2** | White (`#FFFFFF`) | Cod Gray (`#121212`) | Dark backgrounds — the most common "FAI dark mode" |
| **Combination 3** | Black (`#010101`) | Pure White (`#FFFFFF`) | White/light backgrounds, print documents |

**Logo color rules:** Mark and logotype must always be the same color. Never mix colors between the chevron and the text. Never place the logo on a busy image background.

### Clearance Zone

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to **1/3 of the logo's height** on all four sides. No other text, logos, or graphic elements within this zone.

### Minimum Sizes

| Logo variant | Digital (px) | Print |
|---|---|---|
| Full lockup (chevron + "Foundation for American Innovation") | 150px | 1 in / 24.5 mm |
| Acronym lockup (chevron + "FAI") | 70px | 0.8 in / 20 mm |
| Logomark only (chevrons) | 21px | 0.24 in / 6 mm |

### Logo Misuse — Never Do These

- Mix colors between mark and logotype
- Use on image backgrounds that make it illegible
- Stretch or warp proportions
- Outline, add drop shadow, add bevel, or apply any stylistic effects
- Use color combinations outside the approved three
- Recreate logotype using live text (always use the locked asset)

## LETTERHEAD SPECIFICATION

Every FAI letter, memo, testimony, and formal correspondence uses this exact letterhead. It must be reproduced consistently — same proportions, same type, same rule, every time.

### Contact Information (canonical)

```
Website:  www.thefai.org
Address:  1255 Union Street NE, Floor 7, Washington, D.C. 20002
```

### Header Structure

The letterhead header has three elements, top to bottom:

**1. Logo**
- Use `FAI-Logo-Assets/Lockup-Black.svg` (letterhead is always on white)
- Left-aligned, flush with the left margin
- Height: 0.35 in (25pt). The lockup's 4.5:1 aspect ratio means it renders ~1.6 in wide at this height.
- Vertically centered in the top margin zone

**2. Contact line**
- Right-aligned, baseline-aligned with the bottom of the logo
- Single line: `www.thefai.org  ·  1255 Union Street NE, Floor 7, Washington, D.C. 20002`
- Typeface: IBM Plex Sans Regular, 8.5pt, Cod Gray (`#121212`)
- Use a centered dot `·` (U+00B7) as the separator, with one space on each side
- Do not stack address and URL on two lines — keep it one line

**3. Orange rule**
- Full width (spanning from left margin to right margin)
- 1pt stroke, International Orange (`#FF4F00`)
- 6pt below the contact line

### Page Margins

```
Top:     0.75 in   (the header lives within this space)
Bottom:  0.75 in
Left:    1.0 in
Right:   1.0 in
```

The logo top edge sits 0.3 in from the top of the page. Body text begins 0.2 in below the orange rule (approximately 1.0–1.1 in from top of page depending on logo/contact rendering).

### Body Typography

Follows the testimony/letter/comments hierarchy:

| Element | Typeface | Size |
|---|---|---|
| Date line | IBM Plex Sans Regular | 10pt |
| Recipient block | IBM Plex Sans Regular | 10pt |
| Salutation | IBM Plex Serif Regular | 11pt |
| Body text | IBM Plex Serif Regular | 11pt, 140% line-height |
| Closing / signature block | IBM Plex Serif Regular | 11pt |
| Footnotes | IBM Plex Sans Condensed Regular | 9pt |

Paragraph spacing: 6pt after each paragraph. No first-line indent.

### Footer

- Full-width orange rule (1pt, `#FF4F00`) 0.2 in above bottom margin
- Below rule, centered: `www.thefai.org` in IBM Plex Sans Regular 8pt, Cod Gray
- No page number on single-page letters. Add centered page number (IBM Plex Sans Regular 8pt) on multi-page documents, to the right of the URL with a `·` separator: `www.thefai.org  ·  Page 2`

### What to Avoid

- Do not center the logo — always left-align
- Do not stack the contact details into two lines in the header; keep them on one line
- Do not omit the orange rules — they are structural, not decorative
- Do not use the Acronym or Logomark-only assets for letterhead; always use the full Lockup
- Do not add color to the background — letterhead is always white

## PAGE LAYOUT & GRID SYSTEM

### Document Grid

FAI documents use a **three-column structure:**
- **Narrow left column** — body text, annotations, explanatory copy
- **Wider right field** — visual examples, specifications, illustration
- Columns separated by a consistent gutter

### Header Bar (on every specification/body page)

Three zones separated by horizontal rules:
- **Left zone:** Section label (e.g., "Logo", "Typography", "Colors") — bold
- **Center zone:** "FAI - Foundation for American Innovation"
- **Right zone:** "Page [number]"

### Page Type Alternation

FAI documents deliberately alternate between two page types:

1. **Dark impact pages (Cod Gray background)** — Full-bleed Cod Gray or International Orange. Used for section openers, major quotes, mission statements. Type is white IBM Plex Serif at large sizes.

2. **Light specification pages (white/near-white background)** — Detailed content, body text, specifications, tables. Type is Cod Gray.

### Section Dividers

Orange full-bleed pages (International Orange background, black Schmalfette Grotesk section number/title) open each major chapter. This is the "volume knob" that signals a new section.

### Logomark as Page Furniture

The double chevron logomark appears in the **bottom-left corner of every page** as a consistent page furniture element. Small size, serving as a recurring brand anchor.

## ILLUSTRATION SYSTEM

FAI's illustration system is its most distinctive visual asset. All illustrations are:
- **Flat** — no gradients, no shadows, no dimensionality
- Inspired by **1960s Swiss design** and bold modernist aesthetics
- Built from a modular set of geometric/organic base shapes

### Shape Vocabulary

- Circles and semicircles
- Pod / lozenge / oval forms (the "hero shape")
- Concentric arcs and rings
- Vertical bar/stripe clusters
- Wave forms
- Eye / vesica piscis shapes

### Illustration Formats

**Banner format** (2:1 landscape, 50+ variants) — Edge-to-edge compositions for web headers, social covers, email banners. Dense, full-bleed, high energy.

**Freestyle format** (1:1 square, 18 variants) — Figure-on-ground compositions for social posts, podcast thumbnails, avatar-scale use. More focused and iconic.

### The "Volume Knob"

Illustrations can range from **monochrome** (black, white, single orange accent) to **full palette** (all five colors). This is the primary mechanism for bridging institutional ↔ startup contexts:
- Turn it down → more monochrome, more authoritative, more formal
- Turn it up → more colors, more energy, more event/social-ready

## DATA VISUALIZATION GUIDELINES

### Chart Color Order (multi-series)

1. International Orange `#FF4F00` — most important data series
2. Celestial Blue `#4997D0` — secondary series
3. Chrome Yellow `#FFA300` — tertiary series
4. Timberwolf / Warm Gray — supporting/comparison data

### Chart Typography

Follows the pentatonic scale exactly:

| Element | Typeface | Size |
|---|---|---|
| Chart title | IBM Plex Serif Bold | 36pt or 21pt |
| Chart subtitle | IBM Plex Serif Bold | 21pt |
| Axis title | IBM Plex Serif Bold | 12pt |
| Data labels | IBM Plex Serif Regular | 11pt |
| Axis labels / footnotes / source | IBM Plex Sans Regular | 10pt |

Source attribution format: `Source: [Organization], [Year]` at 10pt IBM Plex Sans Regular, bottom-left.

### Chart Backgrounds

- Presentations/screens → Cod Gray background with white text, orange accents
- Print/reports → Pure White background with Cod Gray text, orange accents

### Accessibility

- Minimum contrast ratio: WCAG AA (4.5:1 for body text)
- Orange on white (`#FF4F00` / `#FFFFFF`) = 3.9:1 — use for large text (12pt+) only
- White on Cod Gray = 16.8:1 ✅
- Cod Gray on White = 16.8:1 ✅
- Never rely on color alone — use labels, patterns, shapes as well

## VOICE & TONE

**Core register:** Professional, optimistic, authoritative but approachable. Intellectually serious without being stuffy. Edgy without being reckless.

**Tone by context:**
- Research papers, congressional testimony → formal, precise, citation-heavy
- Op-eds, commentary → analytical but accessible
- Social media, event copy → energetic, punchy, can be irreverent

**What FAI is NOT:** A conventional Brookings-style think tank (too stiff), nor a tech startup (too casual). The brand holds both in tension.

## BRAND PERSONALITY SUMMARY (for document compositional decisions)

When making decisions about layout, color weight, or typographic hierarchy, ask: **Does this feel like it belongs in the same world as a nuclear rave at a DC nightclub AND a Copyright Law Symposium with Supreme Court scholars?** If yes, you're in the right zone.

- Use dark backgrounds boldly — don't be timid about full-bleed Cod Gray
- Use International Orange sparingly but decisively — it should feel like a signal flare, not wallpaper
- Let IBM Plex Serif do the heavy lifting for credibility
- Keep Schmalfette Grotesk rare, large, and powerful — it's the brand's exclamation mark
- Prefer clean white space over decorative fill
- Flat geometry over naturalistic illustration
